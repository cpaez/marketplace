WHAT IS AN OPEN SPACE?

Open space (a.k.a. Unconference) is a way to organize participant-driven workshops and conferences. Participants take control over creating, facilitating and attending (or not) sessions during the open space.

WHAT IS SET?

Nothing really. Only rooms and time slots are prepared. Everything else is given by participants.

HOW DO WE START?

We will start with an introduction on what is open space and how to attend. There will be moderators helping to set up sessions and make sure all works smoothly.
Then we will set up the session board. Everyone will be able to post something to the board. Some subjects will surely be waiting as well – you can propose a subject even before the open space starts.

IF I PROPOSE A SESSION, DO I HAVE TO CONDUCT IT?

NO – and that’s one of the most important rules. If you want to talk about something, you appear in a certain place and talk about it.
DO I HAVE TO SPEAK IN THE SESSION I’M ATTENDING?
NO – you can only listen if you prefer.
DO I HAVE TO STAY FOR THE WHOLE SESSION?
NO – the rule of two feet says: If you’re not learning nor contributing, leave.
Even if you initiated this session.

WHAT HAPPENS IN THE END?

The person guiding the session sums it up and brings findings to a common place, where they get posted for all to see.

ROLES:

Facilitator – someone experienced with open spaces, will be happy to help
Initiator – someone who put the subject up. He or she wants to talk about it
Bumblebee – someone visiting sessions for short periods, cross-pollinating ideas
Butterfly – beautiful and silent – only listens and learns
Contributor – someone actively participating in the session

RULES:

Whoever comes is the right people – if you want to talk about it, please do. If you want to listen, please do.
Whenever it starts is the right time – you don’t have to wait – start right away
Wherever it is, is the right place - you pick a spot
When it’s over, it’s over – you don’t have to artificially fill the time slot. If you finish early – finish it. If you want to continue – initiate another slot
The rule of two feet: If you’re not learning nor contributing, leave.
